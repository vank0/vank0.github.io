Settings

Protocol “auto” picks from the device name and GATT layout — the log shows what it chose. Prints garbled or stops mid-job? Lower chunk size, raise delay, or try the other write mode. Pocket/cat printers are always 384 dots; the paper setting only affects ESC/POS.

Troubleshooting
  • Printer not in the chooser — it is Classic-only, powered off, or already connected to a phone. Unpair it elsewhere first.
  • Connects, “sent N bytes”, but nothing prints — wrong protocol for the device. Switch Protocol in Settings (pocket ↔ cat ↔ ESC/POS) and check the auto-detect line in the log.
  • Connects but no writable characteristic — not a serial-style BLE printer; check the GATT dump in the log.
  • Prints garbage — protocol mismatch; try the other modes.
  • Stops mid-print / missing lines — chunks arrive too fast; lower chunk size, raise delay, or switch write mode to “with response”.
